Facts about dogs

  • Dogs were likely the first animals to be domesticated by humans, over 15,000 years ago.
  • There are over 340 recognized dog breeds worldwide, each with its own unique characteristics.
  • Dogs come in a wide range of sizes, from the tiny Chihuahua to the massive Saint Bernard.
  • Dogs have an incredible sense of smell, estimated to be tens of thousands to even 100,000 times more sensitive than humans.
  • Dogs are known for their emotional intelligence and ability to form strong bonds with humans.
  • Dogs communicate not only through barking but also through body language, facial expressions, and vocalizations.
  • Dogs are highly trainable and can learn a variety of commands and tasks.
  • Throughout history, dogs have served humans in various roles, including hunting companions, guardians, and therapy animals.
  • Dogs have several unique physical traits, such as their ability to sweat only through their paw pads.
  • While the average lifespan of a dog is around 10 to 13 years, there is significant variability depending on factors such as size and breed.
